# Case Study: Phishing Attack

## Overview
Phishing is a common cybercrime in which attackers impersonate trusted organizations to steal sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or OTPs. It is usually carried out through emails, SMS messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate.

## Scenario
In a realistic scenario, a college student receives an email that appears to be from their bank, warning that their account will be suspended due to suspicious activity. The message urges immediate action and includes a link to “verify” account details.

## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The attacker sends a fake email or message posing as a bank or service provider.  
2. The message creates urgency or fear to prompt quick action.  
3. The victim clicks on the link provided.  
4. The link opens a fake website that closely resembles the official one.  
5. The victim enters login credentials and OTP.  
6. The attacker captures the information and accesses the victim’s account.

## Target Audience
Phishing attacks often target students, professionals, and elderly individuals who may not carefully verify sources or links before responding.

## Consequences
Victims can face financial loss, unauthorized transactions, identity theft, and compromised personal accounts. Recovery can be difficult and time-consuming.

## Prevention
Users should check email authenticity, avoid clicking suspicious links, use strong passwords, and enable two-factor authentication to protect themselves.
